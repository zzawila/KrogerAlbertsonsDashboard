Kroger and Albertsons:
A State-by-State Breakdown

The largest grocery merger in history proposes to bring together nearly 5,000 supermarkets under one company's ownership. What does that look like in each state?

By Zachary Zawila
March 17, 2024

The two largest supermarket chains in the United States have announced a merger. The Kroger Company, based in Cincinnati, OH, has proposed to acquire Albertsons Companies, of Boise, ID. But it's far from certain the merger will go through.

On January 15, 2024, the Attorney-General of Washington State sued to block the merger, citing antitrust concerns. Colorado sued on February 14, and on February 26, the Federal Trade Commission, eight states, and Washington, DC also sued in a joint lawsuit.

Politicians from both parties, business representatives, and unions have indicated their opposition to the merger, while a few politicians and even one union have endorsed the merger.

In an effort to allay antitrust fears, Kroger and Albertsons announced a plan to sell over 400 supermarkets to Keene, NH-based grocery wholesaler C&S Wholesale Grocers. Even that may not be enough, though, as there are concerns about C&S's long-term ability to run supermarkets. They currently own and operate just 23 supermarkets. The FTC says in their lawsuit that the divested stores would be insufficient.
